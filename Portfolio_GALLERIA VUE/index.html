Pensieri Verticali
by Morton Feldman, Adelphi editions

Ba thesis project, mentor Mario Piazza

The task at hand was to make a visual version of the book ‘Pensieri Verticali’ by the composer Morton Feldman. This book presents a vast collection of the composer’s essays and three notes by other authors. The notes are included in the form of attachments on the third cover. The project aims to reveal the intellectual and contemplative nature of Feldman’s writings. At the same time, the layout of the text is intended to carry the reader towards an analysis of his words, while suggesting its content through a carefully composed typography.

Typograhpy

and the spread of modern thought (very briefly)

The work is the result of a reflection on the sentence: Typography is what language looks like, by Ellen Lupton. Language has its origins in oral communication and it only finds written form later in history. Occidental thinking is based on the practice of writing, as Walter J. Ong states in his essay Orality and Literacy, and typography has always been the discipline through which writing finds its optimal form and clarity. Therefore the noble technology of typography has had a fundamental importance in giving birth to Modernity, intended as the product of occidental thinking.
